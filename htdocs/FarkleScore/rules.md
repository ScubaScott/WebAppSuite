# Farkle
A push-your-luck dice game for two or more players where the goal is to be the first to score 10,000 points. 
Players take turns rolling six dice, setting aside scoring combinations, and deciding whether to bank their points or risk rolling again to build a larger score.
Basic ScoringPoints are awarded for specific single dice and combinations. 

 *Note: All scoring combinations must come from a single roll; you cannot combine dice from multiple rolls to make a combo.*

  ## Scoring*
|dice|score|
|----|----|
|Single 1|100 points|
|Single 5|50 points|
|Three of a Kind|Face value × 100 (e.g., three 2s = 200, three 6s = 600)|
|Three 1s|1,000 points|
|Four of a Kind|1,000 points|
|Five of a Kind|2,000 points|
|Six of a Kind|3,000 points|
|Straight (1-2-3-4-5-6)|1,500 points|
|Three Pairs|1,500 points|

## How to Play
1. The Turn
  At the start of your turn, roll all six dice. Look for scoring dice. 
  You must set aside at least one scoring die from your roll.
1. The Re-roll
  After setting aside your scoring dice, you have a choice:
    Bank: You can stop rolling and record your points for that turn on the Farkle Score Sheet.
      Your turn ends, and play passes to the left.
    Risk/Roll Again: You can roll the remaining dice that were not set aside. 
      If you score on this new roll, you add those points to your turn's running total. 
      You can then choose to stop or roll again.
1. Hot Dice
  If you successfully score with all six dice in a single turn, this is called "Hot Dice".
  You pick up all six dice and roll again, adding to the total points you have already accumulated for that turn.
1. Farkle (Busting)
  If you roll the dice and none of them can be scored, you have a "Farkle".
  You lose all accumulated points for that turn, and your turn immediately ends.
  
## Winning the Game
  You must usually reach a required threshold (such as 500 or 1,000 points) to "open" the board and start keeping score.
  The first player to reach or exceed 10,000 points triggers the end of the game.
  Every other player gets one final turn to try and score more points than the leader to steal the win.
